Fills cavities, cycle, utilization & tonnage only when the drawing values (volume, wall, projected area), a resin blend, runner %, and annual volume are all set.
• Cycle = wall overhead (14 / 11 / 9s for wall <1.8 / <3.5 / ≥3.5mm) + shot-mass bonus (0-18s by total shot weight) + k × wall² cooling. Source: Rosato §6.
• Cavities = clamp(max(throughput_need, size_min, volume_min), 1, size_max). Sources: SPE Plastics Engineering, Boothroyd-Dewhurst DFMA.
• Tonnage = max(shotG × 1.0, silhouette × cav × 0.85 T/cm²) × 1.15 safety. Uses face-projected silhouette (the actual plastic cross-section accounting for holes / hollows), not the bbox face — critical for hollow parts where bbox over-counts by 2-5×. Sources: Rosato §7.4, Plastics Tech "Sizing the Press".

Methodology & references

↑ Total (with tooling)

total=base+toolAmortPP

Final per-part cost. Tooling amortization is added on top of the marked-up base; once tooling is paid off it drops to zero and total = base.

↑ Base (ex-tooling)

mfgSub=matTotal+machTotal+laborTotal+secMachPP+ohTotal+pkg+freight totalBeforeMargin=mfgSub×(1+duty+sga) base=totalBeforeMargin×(1+margin)

Standard cost waterfall: manufacturing subtotal → landed (duty + SG&A) → marked-up. Duty and SG&A both hit mfgSub, not each other.

  • USITC Harmonized Tariff Schedule (duty lookup)

↑ Tooling amortization

toolLifeParts=toolLife×cavities toolAmortRaw={0if cumulativeVolume ≥ toolLifePartstoolingCosttoolLifePartsotherwise toolAmortPP=toolAmortRaw×(1+scrapRate)

Even amortization across the tool's rated life in shots, multiplied by cavities to convert to parts. Scrap multiplier accounts for parts lost to defects.

  • Plastics Technology — tooling cost & mold life reference

↑ Resin cost

runnerG=runnerOverrideif set, elsepartWeightG×runnerPct100 grossG=partWeightG+runnerG×(1−regrindRate) resinPP=grossG1000×resinCostPerKg

Net virgin resin per part = part weight plus the un-recycled portion of runner. regrindRate is the fraction of runner reground and reused; at 1.0 the runner is free, at 0.0 the full runner is consumed.

  • Plastics Exchange — spot resin pricing

↑ Processing cost

pph=3600cycleTimeSec×utilization×cavities machinePP=machineRatepph directLP=laborRatepph secLP=laborRate×secOpsMin60 secMachPP=secMachineRate×secOpsMin60 processing=machinePP+directLP+secLP+secMachPP

Parts/hr drives machine and direct labor; secondary ops add a flat per-part time. utilization defaults to 0.85 — accounts for changeovers, breaks, and minor stoppages.

  • US BLS — molding operator wages (OES 51-3011)

↑ Scrap cost

matScrap=resinPP×scrapRate machScrap=machinePP×scrapRate laborScrap=(directLP+secLP)×scrapRate scrap=matScrap+machScrap+laborScrap

Modeled as a multiplicative uplift on each cost bucket. Note: this is slightly looser than the textbook approach of pph × (1 − scrapRate) good parts; results are within ~1% for scrap < 5%.

↑ Overhead cost

ohRate=ohOverride100if set, elseregion.ohRate ohTotal=(directLP+secLP)×ohRate

Labor-based overhead burden — not applied to machine cost. If ohRate represents a plant-wide overhead, this will under-allocate to machine-heavy parts vs. a full conversion-cost basis.

↑ Profit (contribution margin)

marginRate=marginOverride100if set, elseregion.marginRate profit=(mfgSub+duty+sga)×marginRate

Margin applied to landed cost. Region defaults: USA 10%, Mexico 9%, China 8%, Indonesia 8%.

↑ Machine rate ($/hr)

bracket=first {tonnage ≤ maxTons} in TONNAGE_BRACKETS machRate=machineRateOverrideif set, elsebracket.baseUSA×region.multiplier

Tonnage brackets (baseUSA $/hr): 50t→$32, 100t→$42, 200t→$52, 400t→$68, 700t→$88, 1000t→$110, >1000t→$140. Region multipliers: USA 1.00, Mexico 0.615, China 0.538, Indonesia 0.364.

↑ Resin blend (cooling coefficient k)

kblend=Σ weighti×kiΣ weighti+fillerpct10×bumpfiller cycleTimeSec=5+kblend×wall²

Linear weight-fraction mix of base resin cooling constants, with a small additive bump per 10% filler loading. Used only inside auto-estimate to derive cycle time — it doesn't affect resin cost (that runs off resinCostPerKg) or any other downstream cost. The override-k escape hatch bypasses the math for highly engineered alloys (PC/PBT, PPS, LCP, etc.) where linear mixing underestimates cooling.

Base resin k (s/mm²): ABS 2.5, PP 2.0, HDPE 2.2, PS 2.3, PC 3.2, PA6 2.7, PA66 2.6, POM 3.0, PET 2.8, PMMA 3.5, PBT 2.6, TPU 2.6, TPE 2.4.

Filler bumps per 10% loading: glass fiber +0.15, talc +0.10, mineral +0.10, CaCO₃ +0.08.

Accuracy: ±10–15% for commodity blends and lightly-filled engineering resins. Highly engineered alloys (e.g. PC/PBT, PPS, LCP) will read low — use the override.

  • Osswald — Injection Molding Handbook (cooling time fundamentals)
  • Plastics Technology — filler effects on cooling

Auto-estimate (cavities, cycle, utilization, tonnage)

utilization=0.85 k=kblendif a resin/blend is set, else2.5 cycleTimeSec=5+k×wall² cavities=snap(annualVolume×cycleTimeSec3600×utilization×4000) tonnage=snap(partWeightG×cavities×(1+runnerPct÷100))

Cycle time follows the standard cooling-time approximation (cooling ∝ wall²) plus ~5s fill/eject. The cooling coefficient k comes from the resin blend when set (see blend math ↑); otherwise it falls back to 2.5 as a commodity-resin default. Cavities sizes the tool to produce annualVolume parts in a 4,000 hr/yr production window. Tonnage uses the ~1 t/g shot-weight rule of thumb. All four snap to common molding values: cavities ∈ {1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64}, tonnage ∈ {50, 100, 200, 400, 700, 1000, 1500}. Confidence: ±15% on cycle time, ±1 cavity step on cavities, ±1 bracket on tonnage. Always validate against a quote when stakes are material.

Regional constants labor, OH, margin, freight, duty, …

Per-region cost defaults. multiplier scales the machine rate bracket; resinPriceFactor scales NA spec-sheet resin prices to local market.

Machine rate brackets USA $/hr by tonnage

Base USA hourly burdened machine rate per tonnage bracket. Regional multiplier (above) scales these. Tonnage value is the bracket ceiling (e.g. 200T row = 150T–200T machines).

Resin database cooling k, density

Per-resin cooling coefficient k (s/mm²) and unfilled density (g/cm³). Cycle ≈ overhead + k × wall². Density drives auto-derived part weight from CAD volume.

Filler effects k bumps, densities

Filler additions to base resin: k bump per 10% raises cooling time; density is used in the volumetric blend rule.
